What the Body Is Doing
🎯 NCLEX Priority
Furosemide always risks hypokalemia — check K⁺ before giving.
Na⁺ follows water — hyponatremia is often dilutional, not depletion.
K⁺ and pH move opposite — acidosis → hyperkalemia; alkalosis → hypokalemia.
Never bolus KCl IV — always diluted, always slow. Direct cardiac toxicity.
Calcium gluconate protects the heart but does NOT lower K⁺.
DKA — K⁺ looks high but crashes after insulin. Replace K⁺ early.
Oliguria <30 mL/hr — always investigate cause before treating.
